1. Easy Pasta Alfredo

Creamy, comforting, absolutely delicious, and super easy. This is the best and most simple recipe for Alfredo sauce that you will find!

  • Time: 15 MINUTES
  • Jump to Ingredients ↓

What Is Alfredo Sauce

Now, some may argue that Alfredo sauce isn’t really Italian- it’s American. I’ll give you a quick history lesson. This sauce was born in Rome from a man named Alfredo. His sauce was a simple emulsification of butter, Parmesan cheese, and pasta. The version we know and love today includes garlic and cream for a saucier consistency. So I guess we can call this modernized sauce an Italian-American lovechild.

Tips for Making the Perfect Alfredo Sauce

  • The sauce will seem thin when you start to mix in the pasta. Don’t worry, just keep tossing. The starches from the pasta will mix into the sauce to give the the perfect consistency. If you use flour or cornstarch to thicken the sauce, it may come out with a gluey consistency once it’s cooled down a bit.
  • You can add a few tsp of Italian seasoning, a pinch of nutmeg or red pepper flakes to add some flare to this sauce. Feel free to get creative with seasoning.
  • You can adjust the ratio of cream to milk in this recipe to suit how rich you’d like it to be.

Ingredient Amount / Notes
Pasta 8 oz (linguini)
Butter 4 tablespoons, unsalted
Garlic 2 cloves, minced
Milk 1.5 cups
Heavy cream 1 cup
Parmesan cheese 0.5 cup, shredded
Salt 0.25 teaspoon (to taste)
Pepper 0.25 teaspoon (to taste)
Fresh parsley 2 tablespoons, chopped

Instructions

1. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions.
2. Melt the butter in a large skillet over medium high heat. Add the garlic and cook for 30 seconds, or until fragrant.
3. Pour in the milk and cream. Stir consistently to avoid burning on the bottom of the pan until the mixture comes to a boil. Turn the heat down to medium, and mix in the parmesan cheese, salt, and pepper. Adjust the seasoning to your own taste.
4. Remove the pan from the heat and mix in the cooked pasta until the sauce begins to thicken. Garnish with parsley, and serve.

2. Classic Tomato Spaghetti

A timeless classic. Rich tomato sauce with fresh basil and a hint of garlic. It's healthy, vegan-friendly, and perfect for a weeknight dinner.

  • Time: 25 MINUTES
  • Jump to Ingredients ↓
Ingredient Amount / Notes
Spaghetti 8 oz
Olive Oil 2 tablespoons
Onion 1 small, finely chopped
Garlic 3 cloves, minced
Canned Tomatoes 1 can (14 oz), crushed
Dried Oregano 1 teaspoon
Fresh Basil Handful, torn
Salt & Pepper To taste

Instructions

1. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook spaghetti until al dente.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add onions and cook until soft (about 5 mins). Add garlic and cook for another minute.
3. Pour in the crushed tomatoes and oregano. Simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
4. Drain the pasta and toss it directly into the sauce. Stir in fresh basil, season with salt and pepper, and serve immediately.

3. Simple Aglio e Olio

The traditional Neapolitan dish of garlic and oil. It uses minimal ingredients but packs a massive punch of flavor.

  • Time: 12 MINUTES
  • Jump to Ingredients ↓
Ingredient Amount / Notes
Spaghetti 8 oz
Olive Oil 0.5 cup, high quality
Garlic 6 cloves, thinly sliced
Red Pepper Flakes 1 teaspoon
Parsley 0.25 cup, chopped
Lemon Juice 1 squeeze (optional)

Instructions

1. Cook spaghetti in salted water. Important: Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. In a large pan, combine olive oil and sliced garlic. Turn heat to medium. Cook slowly until garlic turns golden brown (do not burn!).
3. Remove from heat immediately. Stir in red pepper flakes.
4. Add the drained pasta and the reserved pasta water to the oil mixture. Toss vigorously over medium heat for 1 minute until an emulsion forms. Stir in parsley and serve.
